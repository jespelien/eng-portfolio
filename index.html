Miniaturized High Voltage Power Supply for Electrostatic Precipitation
Description:

Led a team of six undergraduate engineers in the design, optimization, and fabrication of a high-voltage (HV) power supply for an electrostatic precipitator (ESP)-driven facemask, aimed at enhancing portable air filtration efficiency.

Role:

Team Lead at SEAL, responsible for technical leadership, power electronics design, and academic writing.

Key Contributions:
  • Developed a miniaturized HV DC-DC converter using flyback topology, IRFZ44N MOSFET switching, and a Seeeduino-based PWM control system.
  • Integrated a six-stage Cockcroft-Walton voltage multiplier to achieve up to 12 kV output from a 12 V LiPo battery, in a custom, curved PCB package.
  • Optimized electrode geometry and HV supply parameters, resulting in a 25% improvement in filtration efficiency compared to previous iterations (>90% for submicron particles).
  • Oversaw full prototyping cycle, including PCB layout, component selection, manufacturing, and testing using a particle counter.
  • Co-authored a manuscript published at IEEE IECON 2024 and assisted in NSF grant proposal writing.
Challenges:

Fitting a high-voltage power supply and multiplier circuit within the limited volume of a wearable mask required innovative PCB design, component selection, and careful consideration of insulation and creepage distances.

Battery Management System Design – Columbia University Formula Racing
Description:

Designing and prototyping a modular battery management system (BMS) for CUFR’s 2026 electric race car, using Analog Devices ADBMS6830 monitors and ADBMS6822 isoSPI transceivers to manage a 600V, 5.4 kWh lithium-ion battery pack within strict FSAE rules.

Role:

High Voltage Team member, leading BMS hardware and firmware development.

Key Contributions:
  • Designed a daisy-chain configuration for 12 ADBMS6830 monitors (2 per battery segment, 12 cells each) for scalable voltage and temperature monitoring.
  • Selected components for intermediary circuitry (isolated power supplies, optocouplers, and fault protection) to meet FSAE safety standards.
  • Programmed a Texas Instruments C2000 microcontroller in Simulink/Embedded C to establish SPI communication, process cell data, and implement error-handling routines.
  • Created schematics in Altium Designer for ADBMS6830/6822 integration, high-voltage isolation barriers, and CAN FD communication layers; currently laying out PCB for fabrication.
Challenges:

Developing firmware in Simulink from scratch with little documentation; balancing FSAE safety, cost, and space constraints; debugging SPI timing mismatches between C2000 and ADBMS6830.

EV Conversion Project – Electric Vehicle Drivetrain and BMS Integration
Objective:

Convert a 2000 Mazda Miata to a fully electric drivetrain, integrating custom battery configuration, battery management, and high-voltage drive systems within the constraints of a high school garage environment.

Role:
  • Designed and fabricated custom battery enclosures using Inventor and CNC machining.
  • Selected and installed BMS, repaired faulty SMD components, programmed for voltage/temperature monitoring and fault detection.
  • Built wiring harness and integrated with other vehicle components.
  • Integrated CAN bus for real-time data exchange between BMS, charger, motor controller, and dashboard.
  • Bench-tested HV system and performed initial drive tests to validate system integration.
Results:
  • Vehicle successfully operated under electric power, completing multiple test drives.
  • Batteries safely and reliably charge and discharge.
  • Achieved reliable CAN communication across all major subsystems.
